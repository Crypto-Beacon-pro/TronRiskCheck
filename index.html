What is the difference between direct and indirect TRON sanctions exposure?

Direct TRON sanctions exposure means the checked wallet address appears directly on a sanctions list (e.g., OFAC SDN). Indirect exposure means the TRC20 wallet has transacted with a sanctioned entity within the analyzed transaction graph (up to 10 hops). Indirect exposure is classified by hop distance, USDT TRC20 amount, and fund flow direction, enabling proportional compliance decisions on TRON transactions.

What is a TRON address AML risk score?

A TRON address AML risk score is a composite numerical score from 0 to 100 that quantifies the money laundering risk associated with a specific TRC20 wallet or USDT TRC20 address. A score of 0 indicates a completely clean TRON wallet with no risk indicators detected. Scores above 60 indicate high risk requiring compliance action. The score is calculated from 500+ on-chain risk signals specific to the TRON blockchain.

Can I check if a TRON address is on the OFAC sanctions list?

Yes. Tron Risk Check performs real-time OFAC sanctions screening for every TRON address and transaction. The platform checks against the full OFAC SDN List and Consolidated Sanctions List, including crypto address entries specific to the TRON network and USDT TRC20 wallets that have been sanctioned. Results are updated in real time as OFAC publishes new designations.

How does TRON AML compliance differ from Ethereum AML?

TRON AML compliance shares similarities with Ethereum AML (both involve EVM-compatible token ecosystems) but has TRON-specific risk patterns: the high volume of USDT TRC20 makes it a preferred asset for sanctions evasion; TRON's low transaction fees enable rapid layering through many small transactions; TRON's account model and energy/bandwidth system affect how transactions should be analyzed versus Ethereum's gas model.

What is USDT TRC20 AML compliance?

USDT TRC20 AML compliance refers to Anti-Money Laundering verification for Tether (USDT) transactions on the TRON blockchain. Since USDT TRC20 is the most widely used stablecoin for high-volume cross-border transfers, it is subject to heightened AML scrutiny. Tron Risk Check's USDT TRC20 AML platform specifically maps USDT fund flows through the TRON network to detect sanctions exposure, mixer usage, and financial crime indicators.

How many TRON blocks does Tron Risk Check index?

What is the TRON Travel Rule and does Tron Risk Check support it?

The TRON Travel Rule refers to FATF Travel Rule requirements applied to TRON-based VASP-to-VASP transfers, particularly for USDT TRC20 transactions above the FATF threshold ($1,000/€1,000). Tron Risk Check Enterprise plans include dedicated Travel Rule compliance workflow tools that help TRON VASPs identify counterparty VASPs, collect required originator and beneficiary information, and produce Travel Rule-compliant documentation for regulatory reporting.

Can Tron Risk Check identify TRON ransomware wallets?

Yes. Tron Risk Check maintains a continuously updated database of TRON wallets associated with known ransomware groups that demand payment in USDT TRC20 or TRX. When a scanned TRON address has received from, sent to, or is otherwise connected to a ransomware wallet, this is flagged as a high-risk indicator in the AML risk score, with full tracing information showing the fund path through the TRON blockchain.

How does Tron Risk Check handle TRON account activation and AML?

TRON accounts require activation (minimum 1 TRX fee) before they can transact. Tron Risk Check tracks TRON account activation patterns as part of its risk analysis — for example, mass activation of new TRC20 wallets in rapid succession is flagged as a structuring risk indicator. The platform treats TRON account activation transactions as part of the full on-chain risk graph for a comprehensive AML picture.
